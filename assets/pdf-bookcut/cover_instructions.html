Make Your Own
Handbound Book

Kingdom Builders AI Playbooks
Print → Cut each sheet in half → Stack right halves under left → Bind the spine
1
Print all pages on standard 8.5 x 11" paper (landscape). Each sheet has two book pages side by side.
2
Cut each sheet in half vertically down the center using a paper cutter or scissors. You now have two 5.5 x 8.5" half-sheets per page.
3
Separate into two stacks: all LEFT halves and all RIGHT halves. Then place the entire RIGHT stack underneath the LEFT stack, keeping page order.
4
Align all pages, tap on a flat surface to even the edges, then bind along the left spine edge.

What You Need

Standard 8.5 x 11" printer paper (or 24lb for thicker feel)
Paper cutter or sharp scissors
PVA bookbinding glue (Lineco Neutral pH, ~$8 on Amazon)
Two binder clips or a heavy book for clamping while glue dries
Optional: cardstock (65lb+) for front and back covers

Binding Options

Perfect Bind (easiest): Clamp pages together, roughen the spine edge with sandpaper, apply PVA glue in thin layers (2 coats, 30 min between). Let dry overnight. This is how most paperback books are made.

Staple Bind: For shorter playbooks (under 40 pages), use a long-arm stapler along the spine. Fast and durable.

Where to get supplies: Amazon ("bookbinding kit"), Blick Art Materials, or your local craft store (Michaels, Hobby Lobby). Lineco PVA Adhesive is the gold standard for DIY book glue.

Pro Tip: Custom Covers Print the first page of the standard PDF (the cover art) on cardstock (65 80lb) for a sturdy front cover. Print a blank or the footer scripture on cardstock for the back. Fold a strip of cardstock around the spine to complete the binding.
