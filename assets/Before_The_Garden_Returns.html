Three Bold Claims

The interior architecture of a human is a trinity, not a list. Three pillars hold up a life: an Identity received from a Source outside yourself, a Stability built only through voluntary suffering and growth, and an Execution that produces fruit even when fruit is no longer required. You cannot remove any one without the other two collapsing.

Eden was a post scarcity world. So is the world being built right now. Both worlds expose the same truth: when external pressure is removed, the only thing holding a human together is the trinity inside. Right now you can hide a broken pillar behind your work, your bills, your busyness. In five years you will not be able to.

Chapter One
01
The Garden That Returns
Eden was the first post scarcity world. The pattern is repeating.

Read Genesis 2 with fresh eyes. Strip away the centuries of stained glass and theological cliche, and pay attention to the actual conditions of the place described. There is no scarcity. There is no curse on labor. The ground produces food without resistance. The man does not work to survive. He works because he was made to work. The garden does not need him in order to exist. He needs the garden in order to be himself.

This is the part most people miss. Eden was not a paradise of leisure. It was a paradise of meaningful work without survival pressure. Adam was given an identity (named, blessed, given a vocation), placed into a relational covenant with the Source, given material that needed his attention (the garden, the animals, eventually Eve), and asked to produce fruit that was not required for him to live. The whole arrangement is the original picture of abundance with assignment.

Scene

The First Morning

The man wakes in a place that does not require him. The fruit is already on the trees. The water already runs. No predator pursues him. No hunger drives him. He could lie down forever and the garden would continue without him.

He gets up anyway. He has been given names to give. He has been given a place to tend. He has been told who he is by the One who made him, and the telling settled into him like a foundation. He is not chasing food. He is not building shelter. He is doing the only thing left when nothing is required: he is becoming who he was made to be.

Then the serpent arrives. The serpent does not offer him food. He already has food. The serpent offers him an identity he does not have to receive. "You will be like God." Identity by self construction instead of identity by gift. The first move of the Fall is not action. It is the rejection of received identity.

Hold that scene. The first crisis in human history was not scarcity. It was the inversion of received identity into constructed identity in a world that did not need anything else from him. The Fall happened in abundance, not in lack. That is the first thing you have to know about the world being built around you right now.

What is happening with artificial intelligence and robotics is, at the trajectory level, the rebuilding of Eden's conditions without rebuilding Eden's communion. Survival pressure is being lifted, slowly at first and then suddenly. The labor curse is being inverted. Disease is being rolled back. Information is becoming instantly available. Material goods are becoming abundant. Within decades, for billions of people, the line between I work because I have to and I work because I want to will dissolve.

And the same crisis Adam faced will land on every one of those people. Without survival pressure, the question of who you are is no longer answered for you. Without the labor curse, the question of why you are doing anything is no longer answered for you. The garden returns and finds out whether you ever became the kind of person who could live in it.

Visualize It

The Garden Returns

Picture the world ten years out. The work that defined your father's identity is automated. The job category that organized your week is unrecognizable. The shopping mall is gone. The grocery store delivers without a clerk. Your medical care is more accurate than any doctor. Your education is bespoke and free. Your tools build for you while you sleep.

You wake up. The day is yours. Nothing requires you.

The room above your head is enormous and unstructured. There are no walls hemming you in. Some people will use that room to become saints. Some will use it to disintegrate. The difference between the two has nothing to do with how much money is in the room. It has to do with whether the trinity inside the person can hold the room without collapsing it.

When the room opens above you, the trinity inside you has to be ready to fill it. Most people are not.

This Is Already Happening

You may be tempted to read all of this as future tense. It is not. The garden conditions are arriving in waves, and the early waves are already here. Anyone who has retired with enough money to never work again has tasted it. Anyone who built a business that runs without them has tasted it. Anyone who cashed out, sold, inherited, or simply earned enough has tasted it. And the data on what happens to those people is the same as the data on what happens in Eden.

Lottery winners suffer disproportionate rates of bankruptcy, addiction, divorce, and suicide. Trust fund recipients struggle with depression and aimlessness at rates that scale with the size of the fund. Retirees experience rapid cognitive decline when purpose is not replaced. Aristocracies, across every culture in history, decay into decadence within three generations. The pattern does not vary. Abundance without trinity is not paradise. It is exposure.

Chapter Two
02
The Three Pillars
A trinity, not a list. Remove any one and the other two collapse.

Every human, in every era, in every condition, has to carry three things into the garden to actually live there. Not five. Not seven. Three. They are simultaneous, not sequential. You do not graduate from one to the next. They operate at the same time, every day, for the entire length of your life. Lose one and the other two cannot compensate for it indefinitely. They will compensate for a while. That is what makes the imbalance dangerous: the system feels like it is working, right up until the moment it collapses.

This chapter introduces the three. The next three chapters give each one a single pass. The next three playbooks in the series take each one apart and rebuild it slowly. For now, the goal is recognition, not mastery.

Pillar One
The Source
Identity received, not constructed

Who you are, given to you from outside yourself. Source is the root system. Prayer, vocation, covenant, scripture, the names spoken over you by the One who made you. Source is what holds when achievement disappears, applause stops, and the work no longer answers the question of your worth.

Pillar Two
The Soil
Stability through pruning, not equilibrium

How you grow. Soil is the medium that gets turned, broken, watered, fertilized. Pain processed. Trauma healed. Comfort refused so the muscle does not atrophy. Stability is not the absence of disturbance. It is the capacity to absorb disturbance without losing your center.

Pillar Three
The Fruit
Execution as discretion, not necessity

What you produce. Fruit is the visible output of a life rooted in Source and refined in Soil. Action, service, creation, stewardship, generation. In a world where work is required for survival, fruit is partially confused with paychecks. In abundance, fruit becomes pure. It reveals what you actually wanted to make of your life.

Why Trinitarian, Not Modular

Frameworks that look like this trinity exist in every wisdom tradition. Be / Do / Have. Body / Mind / Spirit. Logos / Pathos / Praxis. The pattern repeats because it is real. But many of those frameworks treat the three as options you can specialize in. You pick which one matters most to you and over invest there. The result, every time, is a person who is good at one thing and slowly broken in the others.

The pillars are not modular. They are trinitarian. Remove any one of them, and the other two cannot hold the structure together. The early failure looks like compensation: you double down on Execution because Identity is hollow, you double down on Identity because the Soil has gone unworked, you double down on Soil because there is no Fruit to show for the grinding. Each compensation feels productive. None of them are. The system is just buying time.

The Modular Lie

Pick Your Strongest. Specialize.

  • "My identity work is solid. I'll just keep producing."
  • "I work hard, that's enough."
  • "I pray, that's the foundation. The action follows."
  • "I'm growing through hard things. That covers me."
The Trinitarian Truth

All Three. Always. At Once.

  • Source feeds Soil. Soil refines Fruit. Fruit feeds back to Source.
  • Strong in two and weak in one is still a broken system.
  • The strong pillars hide the broken one until the conditions change.
  • When abundance arrives, the hiding place is removed.

The simplest test: imagine each pillar removed in isolation. Notice that none of the remaining configurations describe a thriving life. They all describe a recognized failure mode. That recognition is the entire point of the trinitarian frame. You cannot specialize your way around this.

The Eden Architecture

In the garden, all three pillars were operating simultaneously and visibly. Adam's identity was given by God (Source). Adam was being formed through naming, recognition, and the surgery that produced Eve (Soil). Adam was tending and cultivating (Fruit). The Fall did not begin when one pillar was removed. The Fall began when one pillar was substituted with a counterfeit.

The serpent did not destroy Adam's identity. The serpent offered Adam a constructed identity to replace the received one. The work continued. The growing continued. But the Source had been swapped, and within a single conversation, all three pillars were rotted. That is how fast it goes when the trinity is not protected.

The trinity is not a goal. It is a load bearing structure. Most people discover this only when the load shifts and the missing pillar becomes the reason the building falls.

Chapter Three
03
The Source
Pillar One. Identity received, not constructed.

Every human knows who they are by some answer to one of two questions. The first question is: who told you who you are? The second is: what have you done that proves who you are? The first answer is received. The second answer is constructed. Most people in the modern world live almost entirely on the second answer, and they do not know it because the first answer has gone quiet under the noise of their accomplishments.

Received identity is who you are when no one is watching, no one is paying you, and nothing is being produced. It is the residue that remains after every external marker is stripped. Constructed identity is who you are because of what you have built, signaled, or performed. Both are real. But only one of them survives abundance, and abundance is what is coming.

Scene

The Founder Who Sold

A man builds a company for sixteen years. Wakes early. Sleeps late. Misses things. Becomes the title on his email signature. Becomes the role he holds at dinner parties. Becomes the authority his wife has stopped questioning because she knows he will not back down.

He sells. The check clears. He wakes the next Tuesday with no calendar.

By Friday he is anxious. By the third month he is depressed. By the sixth month he is having an affair he does not want, drinking more than he meant to, and looking for a new company to start before the silence eats him alive. He had built an identity that required the work to keep existing. When the work stopped, the identity began to dissolve. He could not feel himself anymore.

His pastor asked him a question over coffee: "Who are you when no one is paying you?" The man could not answer. The question did not have an answer because he had never asked it.

This pattern is not rare. It is the modal experience of post acquisition founders, retirees from high status careers, athletes after the league, performers after the spotlight, and parents after the kids leave. The constructed identity dissolves the moment the construction site is closed. People with received identity do not have this problem in the same way. They grieve the loss of role. They do not lose themselves.

This is why prayer is non negotiable in this framework. Prayer is not a mood. Prayer is the daily practice of receiving identity from a Source outside the self. It is the deliberate, regular, disciplined act of letting yourself be told who you are by the One who made you, instead of telling yourself who you are by reviewing your portfolio.

Why Source Holds Under Abundance

A self constructed identity has nothing to push against in crisis. The materials it was built from (achievement, recognition, role) are exactly the materials that disappear when the conditions change. It is built from the very thing that gets removed.

A Source rooted identity has a fixed point outside the storm. The materials it was built from (the words of God, the covenant relationships, the vocation given before any performance) are not affected by external conditions. The work can stop. The applause can fade. The body can fail. The Source does not move.

This is the same reason ancient saints could die well, and modern celebrities often cannot live well. It is not a difference in personality. It is a difference in the architecture of identity.

Stop and Locate Your Source

If a stranger asked you who you are, your honest first answer would probably be your job title or your role. That is the constructed answer. Now answer the deeper version:

Who told you who you are, and when did they tell you? Can you say it out loud right now without referencing anything you have built?

This pillar gets a full playbook in Part 2 of this series. The Source. For now, simply hold the diagnostic question in your hand. The answer is the entire test of whether your Identity will survive what is coming.

Chapter Four
04
The Soil
Pillar Two. Stability through pruning, not equilibrium.

Notice the first paradox. You will use the word stability in this framework, but you will not mean what most people mean by it. Most people use stability to mean calm. Equilibrium. The absence of disturbance. They want their lives to be a flat surface. The wisdom traditions, the agricultural metaphors, the actual experience of every person who has ever grown into someone strong, all teach the opposite. Real stability is the capacity to absorb disturbance without losing your center. It is built only by being disturbed.

Soil is the metaphor because soil tells the truth. Soil that is never turned, never broken, never amended, becomes hardpan. Hardpan looks stable but cannot grow anything. The roots cannot penetrate it. The water runs off it. A plant placed in hardpan dies even with sunlight and seed. The same is true of human stability. The soul that has never been broken is not strong. It is hardpan. The first real disturbance reveals what was actually underneath.

Scene

The Pruning

The vinedresser walks the rows in late winter. He carries a small curved blade. He stops at each plant and cuts. Some cuts are gentle. Some are aggressive. The branches he removes are not dead. Many of them produced fruit last year. Some of them produced the most fruit. He cuts them anyway.

An amateur watches and is disturbed. Why are you cutting the productive ones?

The vinedresser does not look up. "Because if I do not, the vine will exhaust itself feeding too many branches. The fruit will get smaller every year. By year four, the vine will look healthy and produce nothing worth eating. The cuts are not punishment. They are protection."

This is the second pillar. You will not bear real fruit without being cut. If you arrange your life to never feel resistance, never face hard truths, never grieve, never confess, never let anything be taken from you, you will not become stable. You will become hardpan.

This is also where the AI abundance question gets uncomfortable. Most of human history has involved involuntary disturbance. Famine. Disease. Backbreaking work. Sudden death. Brutal weather. Loss of children to childhood illness. The pruning was constant and unchosen. Even those who did not pursue growth got it anyway, because life delivered it to their doorstep without asking.

That is being removed. Not all at once, but on a curve. Material abundance is rising. Health is extending. Comfort is everywhere. The friction that did the involuntary pruning is dwindling. If your stability has been built almost entirely on involuntary suffering, your stability stops being built when the suffering stops arriving. This is one of the largest underdiscussed implications of what is coming.

The first generation in human history is being raised in conditions of near total comfort. Their soil is going unworked. Their roots are shallow. Their tolerance for disturbance has not been built because disturbance has been engineered out of their environment. And then we wonder why anxiety, depression, and identity collapse are climbing in exactly the populations that have the most material abundance.

~30%
Increase in major depressive episodes among Gen Z vs prior generations at the same age
Material conditions improved. The interior conditions did not. The soil was never worked.

The corollary, and this is the hardest sentence in the playbook so far: in abundance, suffering becomes a discipline, not a circumstance. If you do not deliberately install rhythms of voluntary discomfort, fasting, hard physical work, deep relational risk, financial restraint, silence, confession, sacrifice, your soil will go unworked. The amateur reads this and recoils. Why would I voluntarily suffer when I do not have to? Because if you do not, the pillar atrophies. And when real disturbance comes, and it will, you will have nothing to absorb it with.

The Comfort Trap

Hardpan

  • Avoid all unnecessary discomfort
  • Optimize the environment for ease
  • Outsource friction to tools and money
  • Refuse correction and difficult conversations
  • Build a life with no chosen hard edges

Worked Soil

  • Install voluntary discomfort as a weekly rhythm
  • Welcome correction from people you trust
  • Process pain instead of routing around it
  • Refuse comfort that you have not earned through effort
  • Let community prune what you cannot prune yourself
Stop and Examine the Soil

Walk through the last twelve months of your life. When was the last time you were genuinely uncomfortable, and was the discomfort chosen or imposed? When was the last time you let someone tell you something hard about yourself and actually changed in response? When did you last sit with a difficult feeling without immediately routing around it with food, scrolling, drinking, or working?

If the honest answer is "I cannot remember" for any of these, your soil has been going unworked. Pillar Two is the playbook that follows this one.

Chapter Five
05
The Fruit
Pillar Three. Execution as discretion, not necessity.

This is the pillar most people assume they are strongest at, and most people are wrong. Fruit is the visible output of a life. Action. Service. Creation. Stewardship. Generation. The reason most people overrate their fruit is that the current world rewards any output, including output that is not fruit at all. Activity is paid. Productivity is paid. Hustle is paid. None of those are necessarily fruit. Fruit is what you would still produce if no one was paying you to produce it.

That is the test that most current life cannot pass. Take away the paycheck, the recognition, the deadline, the social pressure, and watch what happens. For most people, output collapses to near zero, sometimes for months. They tell themselves they are resting. They are not resting. They are discovering that their entire fruit pillar was being held up by external scaffolding, and the scaffolding is what was keeping them moving.

Scene

The Sabbatical

A woman takes a year off. She has saved enough. The deadline pressure is gone. She tells everyone she is going to write the book she has wanted to write for fifteen years. She has the time. She has the money. Nothing is in her way.

By month three she has not written a chapter. She is reading. She is going on walks. She is "letting it come."

By month nine she is starting to lie about it. "It's coming along. I'm in the deep work phase." She is not. The book is not happening. She had assumed her execution was a muscle. It was a response to pressure. Without pressure, the muscle did not exist.

This is what abundance does to most fruit. It removes the pressure that was doing the work, and the person discovers they were not the engine. The pressure was the engine. They were just along for the ride.

This is why discretionary fruit is the test of the third pillar. In a world where work is required for survival, this test is hidden. Most people never face it. They confuse their job with their fruit because the job answers both questions at once: it pays the bills and it produces output. When the bills stop requiring the job, the output question is exposed for the first time. And many people discover, to their horror, that the output they were generating was not actually theirs.

What does discretionary fruit actually look like? In a world of AI and automation, where machines can produce most of the things humans used to produce for survival, fruit becomes more specific, not less. The forms of fruit that remain meaningfully human are narrower than people assume. They are also exactly the forms that machines cannot generate, because they require something machines do not have, namely a human soul on the producing end.

The Five Forms Of Human Fruit

Relational. Depth of presence with another human. Friendship. Marriage. Mentorship. Showing up at the hospital, the funeral, the wedding, the difficult Tuesday. This cannot be automated because the value of presence is the human behind it.

Creative. Making something whose value is the human behind it. The painting. The song. The book. The dinner. Machines can imitate the form. They cannot generate the soul that wanted to make it.

Servant. Showing up in the suffering of others. Hospice. Foster care. Recovery rooms. The places where a human face is the entire point. Machines cannot substitute here, no matter how sophisticated.

Stewardship. Caring for what cannot be priced. The elderly. The broken. The wild. The beautiful. The forgotten places. The body, the temple you were given to keep. The discipline of preserving things that the market does not value.

Generative. Raising other humans. Children. Disciples. Apprentices. The patient, slow, unautomatable work of forming a person.

These are not the only fruits. They are the ones that remain as the mechanical fruits get automated away. Whatever you build the third pillar from, build it from these.

Stop and Find Your Discretionary Output

If your job stopped tomorrow and your bank account was full enough that you never needed to work again, what would you actually do by the end of the first year? Not what you would say. What you would actually do.

The honest answer is your discretionary fruit. If the answer is "I do not know," you are about to find out, and you will be more comfortable finding out now than later.

The third pillar gets a full playbook in Part 4 of this series. The Fruit. The protocols are concrete. The rebuild is achievable. But it starts with admitting that what you have been calling fruit was, for many of you, just response to pressure dressed up as a life.

Chapter Six
06
The Seven Imbalances
The diagnostic. One of these is your life right now.

Read this chapter slowly. Read it more than once. One of the seven configurations below is describing your life with surgical precision, and you may not recognize which one until you have read all seven. The temptation will be to read each one, decide it is not you, and move on. Resist that. The mode that is your life will feel uncomfortable when you read it, not familiar. Discomfort is the diagnostic signal. Familiarity means you are reading someone else's failure mode.

Each mode below names which pillars are operating and which one is weak. Each gives a one line confession from the person living it. Each describes the eventual collapse. Each gives the immediate failure mode now, in the current world, AND the catastrophic failure mode in the abundance world that is coming. The same imbalance fails differently in different conditions. That is why this needs to be read with both timelines in mind.

Mode One
The Vapid Achiever
Identity weak, Soil and Fruit strong

"I work hard. I grow when work goes badly. I produce. But I do not actually know why I am doing any of it."

Failure mode: Years or decades of high performance with no anchor. Eventually arrives at the top of the ladder and finds it leaning against the wrong wall. Solomon at the end of Ecclesiastes. Mid life crisis is built into this mode.

Now Looks like winning. Achievement is providing enough dopamine to mask the hollowness. Most successful men in their 30s and 40s live here without knowing it.

In Abundance Catastrophic. The dopamine source is automated away. The achievement no longer satisfies because the conditions made it cheap. Identity collapses with no scaffolding to hold it up. This is the most common predicted failure mode in the post AI world.

Mode Two
The Pharisee
Soil weak, Identity and Fruit strong

"I know who I am. I produce. But I have not let anything genuinely change me in years."

Failure mode: Brittle. The identity is real but defended. Correction is rejected. Theology never costs anything. When real crisis comes, the structure does not bend, it breaks.

Now Looks like maturity. Often shows up in successful church people, settled professionals, people who have figured out their identity early and stopped letting it be tested. The hardpan looks like a foundation.

In Abundance Worse. The conditions remove most involuntary disturbance. Without active discipline, the soil hardens completely. The first real shock, even small, will shatter what looks indestructible. Common in retirees who calcify after their last big challenge.

Mode Three
The Contemplative Stuck
Fruit weak, Identity and Soil strong

"I pray. I process. I heal. I have my why. But I do not actually do anything with any of it."

Failure mode: Atrophy. Action muscles waste. Eventually unable to act because the wiring was never built. Therapy as a lifestyle. Faith without works. The talent buried in the field.

Now Often hidden by the lifestyle of growth. The person reads books, attends retreats, goes to therapy, journals, prays. It looks like spiritual seriousness. Externally there is little to show.

In Abundance The trap deepens. The pressure to produce will fall away. The contemplative stuck never had to face the choice of action because they could always say "I'm still working on it." When time is unlimited, "still working on it" can run for the entire rest of your life.

Mode Four
The Dreamer With No Land
Identity strong, Soil and Fruit weak

"I have my calling. I have my vision. I pray. I just have not built anything yet, and I do not allow much to challenge me."

Failure mode: Vision drifts into fantasy. Identity becomes performance because nothing is validating it externally and nothing is refining it internally. Ego inflates. The "anointing" stops being tested by reality.

Now Often shows up in people with strong faith communities, prophetic personalities, or aspirational identities. Beloved by their friends. Producing very little. The story they tell about themselves and the actual measurement of their life are diverging.

In Abundance Severe. Without external pressure, the gap between the story and the reality widens until the person becomes delusional. They will have decades of "almost." Eventually no one believes them anymore, including themselves.

Mode Five
The Permanent Patient
Soil strong, Identity and Fruit weak

"I am always processing. Always healing. Always learning from pain. But I do not really know who I am, and I do not produce much."

Failure mode: Trauma becomes the identity. Healing becomes the lifestyle. The soil is endlessly worked, but nothing is ever planted. The person is always growing and never producing, because there is no settled self to do the producing.

Now Endemic in modern therapy culture. Often disguised as deep work. Real healing is happening, but it never resolves into a built life. The wound has become the person.

In Abundance Becomes terminal. With unlimited time and resources, processing can run forever. The person never has to graduate. Decades pass in self examination that never crosses into self deployment.

Mode Six
The Hustler
Fruit strong, Identity and Soil weak

"I work. I build. I ship. I do not really know why and I do not really grow. I just keep moving."

Failure mode: The most dangerous mode because it looks like success the longest. Eventually the moral collapse, the affair, the breakdown, the addiction, the sudden disqualification. The successful pastor falls. The founder jumps. Identity was the work, and the work cannot bear that weight.

Now The default mode for high performers. The loudest voice in the modern world is "do more, build more, ship more." The hustler is rewarded externally for years. Internally, something is corroding the entire time.

In Abundance Apocalyptic. The work that was holding the person together gets automated. The identity built on output collapses overnight. No Source to fall back on. No Soil to absorb the shock. This is the predicted failure mode of an entire generation of high achievers.

Mode Seven
The Drift
All three weak

"I am not really sure what I'm doing. I am not really anyone in particular. I am not really growing. I am not really producing. I am just here."

Failure mode: The default modern condition for many. Despair, disengagement, depression. Often medicated. Sometimes hidden behind functional employment. The person is alive but not living.

Now Hidden by the structures of work and convention. The job provides a fake identity. The bills provide fake purpose. The schedule provides fake meaning. The drift is masked.

In Abundance The masks fall. Without work, bills, or schedule, the drift becomes visible to everyone, including the person living it. Suicide rates among populations with this configuration historically rise sharply when conditions improve. Abundance kills the drift faster than scarcity does.

The mode you are in is not your destination. It is your starting line. The diagnosis is half of the cure. The other half is the playbook that follows this one.

Here is the temptation, and you will feel it in the next several minutes. The temptation is to read all of the above, decide it was interesting, close the playbook, and not act on it. The Soil playbook takes time. The Fruit playbook takes time. Pillars are not weekend projects. They are decade projects. The people who arrive at abundance with their pillars in place will be the people who started building before the conditions made building obvious.

"What should I do?" the Drifter asked.

"Build now. Build the pillar that is weakest. The strong ones will hold while you work. "And if I wait?"

"You will be a man who began the work in the storm instead of in the sunshine. The work is the same. The conditions are not. The pregame ends when the game starts. The game has started for some of you and is starting for the rest."

The Machine is not the villain in this story. The Machine is the variable that is changing. It is doing exactly what every previous tool did, only at a different speed and a different scope. It is removing what it can remove. What it cannot remove is the trinity inside the human, because the trinity is not a function the Machine can perform. That is the entire opportunity. The Machine is offloading the things that were never the meaning of human life, and leaving the things that are.

This is also why the framework is useful right now, before any of the abundance has arrived. The trinity holds in scarcity, in transition, and in abundance. The diagnostic works in any era. Whatever conditions you find yourself in, the same three pillars are the load bearing structure of your life. The work to build them well is identical regardless of timing. The only thing the abundance does is make the work more visible and the failure more catastrophic.

The window is not a problem. It is a gift. It is the time the framework is asking you to use to build the pillars that will hold when the conditions change. Every year of the window you spend ignoring the diagnostic is a year you cannot get back. The work is the same regardless of when you do it. The only difference is whether you do it in sunshine or in storm.
