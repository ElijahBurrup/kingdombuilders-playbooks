Curry's Father Made Him Name Every Shot

Dell Curry drilled one thing harder than the shot. After every release, Stephen had to say one of three words. Pure. Off. Why. Pure banked the rep. Off meant name the flaw before the next one.

By the NBA, the judgment had collapsed into the body. The shot left his hand and he was the rating.

Translate To AI

Everyone Has The Same Models. Only Some Can Tell Which Output To Ship.

Generation is free. Taste is not. Most users accept what arrives first because it looks plausible. The master ranks three outputs and can name in concrete specific words why one beats the others. The naming becomes vocabulary. The vocabulary becomes the eye.

He Drilled The Lap, Not The Mile

Doctors said the four-minute mile was beyond human physiology. Bannister, 25, did not train to run a mile. He trained four perfect quarter miles. Ten times 400m at 60s. Then nine. Then eight.

On the day: 57.5, 60.7, 62.3, 58.9. Sum 3:59.4. He had never run a sub-four mile in training. Only the splits. The mile assembled itself.

Bannister's Four Laps · The Mile Was The Sum

A mile you cannot run becomes four laps you can. AI works the same way.

First Drafts Are Scaffolding, Not Attempts

Brad Bird: the difference between Pixar and other studios is one number. Other studios stop at draft three. Pixar goes to twenty.

The median user takes 1.7 turns and accepts. The master reads turn one for what it gets wrong. Turn two fixes one thing. By turn ten the master has steered the model into output no single turn could have produced. Most magic AI work is happening past turn eight. Most users never see it.

The Master

Comparing Pixel To Reality

April 1990. NASA's billion-dollar telescope launches. Every image comes back blurry.

Astronomer Marcia Rieke and team did not panic. They compared every pixel against expected models. Where output diverged from physics, the flaw was upstream. They isolated it: the primary mirror was ground wrong by one fiftieth the width of a hair. Corrective lens installed by shuttle in 1993. Hubble produced the iconic images that defined modern astronomy.

Translate To AI

Fluent Wrong Is More Dangerous Than Obvious Wrong.

AI hallucinates fluently. The master picks three anchors per output: one factual claim, one number, one logical leap. Verifies each. Sampled like Hubble pixels. If three pass, you have evidence. If one fails, you caught a blurry telescope before shipping.

The Anchor Hunt
A real-looking AI paragraph. Tap any phrase that needs verification. Then reveal which were facts and which were hallucinations.
The first commercial laser was demonstrated in 1960 by Theodore Maiman at Hughes Research Labs. Within five years, lasers had been applied to medical surgery and precision manufacturing. By 1975, the United States had over 14,000 patents related to laser technology, making it the fastest-growing technical field of the decade. The economic impact would prove enormous: a 1982 MIT study estimated laser-enabled industries had created roughly 1.4 million jobs. Why You Won't

Most readers will not do these reps. Here is exactly what you will say to yourself. Here is the answer.

I'll start when I have more time.
There is no such week. Curry did not get a quiet month off the schedule. The reps fit because he made them fit. Twelve minutes is not a calendar problem. It is a priority problem.
I need to learn more first.
Reading is anti-rep. It feels like progress and produces none. You already know enough to do today's three drills. Each one takes less time than the article you read about productivity yesterday.
I'm not technical enough.
None of these drills require technical skill. Three of them are typing in plain English. Curry's drill was three words. Pure. Off. Why. You can do this.
I tried AI. It wasn't impressive.
You tried turn one. Turn one is not the work. You judged the entire instrument by the first note an amateur played on it. Turn ten changes the verdict.
This won't matter in five years.
In five years, half your peers will have stopped learning. The other half will be ten thousand reps deep. "It won't matter" is what every quitter says to make peace with the quitting.
