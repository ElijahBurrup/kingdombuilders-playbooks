The Master

Curry's Father Made Him Name Every Shot

Dell Curry, an NBA shooter himself, drilled one thing harder than the shot. After every release, Stephen had to say one of three words. Pure. Off. Why. Pure banked the rep. Off meant name the flaw before the next shot.

The Currys were not training the shot. They were training the judgment of the shot. By the NBA, the judgment had collapsed into the body. The shot left his hand and he was the rating.

Amateur vs Master · Same Release. Different Eye.

The amateur cannot tell if the shot is good until the ball lands. The master cannot tell because the body has already rated the release.

Translate To AI

Everyone Has The Same Models. Only Some Can Tell Which Output To Ship.

Generation is free. Taste is not. Most users accept whatever arrives first because it looks plausible. They ship the average. The master ranks three outputs and can name why one beats the others in concrete, specific words. The naming becomes vocabulary. The vocabulary becomes the eye.

The Daily Drill
12 min
The Three Outputs Rating
  1. Pick one real task today. Generate the output three times (same prompt or three different models).
  2. Rank them. Best, middle, worst.
  3. Write one sentence explaining why the best one wins. Use a specific concrete word: not "better tone" but "less hedging," "the example was real," "structure went general-to-specific."
  4. Write one sentence on why the worst one fails. Same rule.
  5. Keep the running list. After 30 days you have 60 specific named virtues and flaws. That list is your taste.
Now Do It

Stop reading. Open your AI. Generate three of anything. Rank them. Come back when the list has its first two entries.

Ten Years On Rice, And The Rice Was Not The Point

Jiro's son Yoshikazu trained under him over a decade. His only job was rice. Wash, measure, cook, season, shape, taste. Repeat. After eight years, Jiro said: "The rice is ready."

What Yoshikazu had built was not a recipe. It was a vocabulary of imperfection. Too soft. Too dry. Vinegar penetration uneven. Grain temperature dropping faster on the surface. Compression artifacts from the thumb. The naming let him see what no untrained eye could see. The skill was the vocabulary. The vocabulary was the skill.

The 25 / 15 / 5 Compression

If you cannot name what you want in five words, you do not know what you want. The model cannot help you find out.

Translate To AI

The Model Only Knows What You Say.

"Email about the project delay" gets a generic email. "90 words, 4-year client, 2-week delay, vendor caused it, full responsibility, specific recovery date, warm but direct, no apologies for apologizing" gets a specific email. Same model. Naming is the leverage.

The Daily Drill
10 min
The 25 / 15 / 5 Funnel
  1. Pick one fuzzy thing you want today. A piece of work, an outcome, a request.
  2. Write it in exactly 25 words. You will discover what you do not yet know.
  3. Compress to exactly 15. Delete the noise, keep the signal.
  4. Compress to exactly 5. That is the name of what you want.
  5. Prompt the model using the 5 as title and the 25 as spec. Where the compression hurt most is where your thinking was fuzziest.
Now Do It

Pick the next thing on your list. Compress it to five words before the next paragraph. Yes, this paragraph.

The Master

He Drilled The Lap, Not The Mile

Doctors said the four-minute mile was beyond human physiology. Hearts would burst. Bannister, a 25-year-old medical student, did not train to run a mile. He trained to run four perfect quarter miles. Ten times 400 meters at 60 seconds, then nine, then eight. The unit. Not the goal.

On the day, his splits were 57.5, 60.7, 62.3, 58.9. Add them: 3:59.4. He had never run a sub-four mile in training. He only ran the splits. The mile assembled itself.

Bannister's Four Laps · The Mile Was The Sum

A "mile" you cannot run becomes four "laps" you can. AI works the same way.

Translate To AI

Do Not Prompt The Project. Prompt The Lap.

"Build me a marketing strategy" is a mile. The output is generic because no model runs a mile in one stride. The master prompts: "List 8 pain points the product solves, one concrete example each." Lap one. The output of lap one becomes the input of lap two. The smallest atomic step is the entire skill.

The Daily Drill
15 min
The Five Step Pipeline
  1. Take any real task currently bigger than one prompt.
  2. Write the goal in one sentence. What does done look like?
  3. Write the smallest first step the AI could fully complete in one turn. Almost embarrassingly small.
  4. Steps two through five each take the prior output as input. Chain them.
  5. Run the pipeline. The bottleneck step is the lap you need to drill again.
Now Do It

Pick the project you have been avoiding because it feels too big. Write step one. The size of step one tells you how stuck you really are.

The Master

First Drafts Are Scaffolding, Not Attempts

Brad Bird directing The Incredibles: the difference between Pixar and other studios is one number. Other studios stop at draft three. Pixar goes to twenty. The films that look effortless took twenty drafts.

In 1998 Pixar screened the first cut of Toy Story 2. It was bad. They threw out the entire film and rebuilt it in nine months. The rebuild grossed half a billion dollars. The rule they understood: turn one is not a real attempt. Turn one is the scaffolding that reveals the shape of the problem.

Where Most Users Stop · Where The Work Is

The bottom half of users average 1.7 turns per task. The work that earns money lives at turn 8 to 12.

Translate To AI

Turn One Is The Spec, Not The Answer.

The median user takes 1.7 turns and accepts. The master reads turn one for what it gets wrong. Turn two corrects. Turn three refines. By turn ten the master has steered the model into producing something the model could not have produced in any single turn. Most magic AI work is happening past turn eight. Most users will never see it.

The Daily Drill
15 min
The Ten Turn Refinement
  1. Take a result you accepted in one turn yesterday. Open a fresh conversation.
  2. Turn 1: original prompt. Generate.
  3. Turns 2–7: one specific fix per turn. Single weakest part. Rewrite.
  4. Turn 8: ask the AI to critique its current output as if grading someone else.
  5. Turn 9: have it incorporate that critique. Turn 10: compare to turn one. The gap is your iteration deficit.
Now Do It

Open the last AI output you accepted too fast. Run five more turns on it. The gap will embarrass you. That is the rep.

The Master

Three Known Things, Six Years In The Combiner

Miranda was on vacation in 2008. He picked up Ron Chernow's biography of Hamilton. By page 30 he was scribbling. The book's premise was that Hamilton was a self-invented immigrant who hustled to power by sheer verbal velocity. Miranda's brain did the strange thing. This man is a hip-hop artist.

Six more years to finish. The shocking thing about Hamilton: no single component was new. Founding Father biographies, hip-hop, R&B, Broadway musicals all existed. Miranda did not invent the parts. He synthesized three known fields into one unknown thing. The synthesis was the originality.

The Three Circles · Where Hamilton Lived

The originality was the overlap. The parts existed before. Nobody had stood where the three crossed.

Translate To AI

Run Three Sources. Combine The Best Of Each.

Most users run one prompt and accept. The master runs three. Different framings or different models. Pulls the single strongest element from each. The synthesis is the original work. Nobody else picked your three highlights. Nobody else made your combination. That is where AI work stops feeling derivative and starts feeling like yours.

The Daily Drill
15 min
The Three Source Combine
  1. Pick one task where you want output that feels original.
  2. Generate three different ways. Prompt A: analytical. Prompt B: as a story. Prompt C: as a list of contradictions.
  3. Highlight the single strongest line or idea from each. One per source.
  4. New prompt: "Combine these three elements into one coherent piece." Paste the highlights.
  5. Edit by hand to make it yours. The final piece is not what any single prompt produced. It is your synthesis.
Now Do It

Pick a piece of writing you have to do today. Run three versions before you finish this page. The combine takes longer than the page.

The Master

Comparing Pixel To Reality

April 1990. NASA's billion-dollar telescope launches. The first images come back blurry. Every single one. The most expensive eye ever pointed at the universe is producing pictures worse than a backyard scope.

Astronomer Marcia Rieke and her team did not panic. They compared every pixel against expected models. Calibration stars whose light output was known. Physics predicting what each pixel should record. Where output diverged from model, the flaw was upstream. They isolated it: the primary mirror had been ground to the wrong shape by one fiftieth the width of a human hair. A corrective lens, installed by shuttle in 1993, and Hubble produced the iconic images that defined modern astronomy.

Before / After · What Verification Made Possible

Plausible is not the same as true. The verification is the difference between magic and embarrassment.

Translate To AI

Fluent Wrong Is More Dangerous Than Obvious Wrong.

AI hallucinates. Confidently. The output looks right because the model is articulate. Fluency is the enemy of verification. The master picks three anchors per output: one factual claim, one number, one logical leap. Verifies each. Not exhaustive. Sampled like Hubble pixels against calibration stars. If three pass, you have evidence. If one fails, you have caught a blurry telescope before you shipped it.

The Daily Drill
10 min
The Three Anchor Check
  1. Before you ship, pick one factual claim. Open a primary source. Click past Wikipedia to the cited source.
  2. Pick one number or statistic. Search for it. If you cannot find an authoritative source in two minutes, the number is suspect.
  3. Pick one logical leap. Would a careful reader agree the step follows? If you must argue for it, the leap is shaky.
  4. If any anchor fails, treat the whole output as suspect.
  5. Log what failed. After 30 days you will know your model's hallucination patterns. That pattern recognition is verification mastery.
Now Do It

The last AI output you sent: pick three anchors right now. Verify them. The thing you do not want to check is exactly the thing you need to check.
