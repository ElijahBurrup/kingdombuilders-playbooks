Chapter One

The Ball & the Scale Architecture

Five overlapping layers. The scales overlap so no single bite can penetrate.
The Clearing at Dusk

The lion bit. Its canines, capable of crushing a zebra's windpipe, slid off the scales. It bit again. Same result. It picked Scale up and dropped her. It batted her with a paw that could disembowel a wildebeest. The scales held. It mouthed her for forty-five minutes. Then the lion got bored. It yawned, stretched, and walked away.

Scale had expended almost zero energy. Her heart rate had barely elevated. She was uninjured.

The pangolin's scales overlap. No single bite finds a gap because each scale is partially under the next one. The defense is not in any single scale. It is in the overlap. Five layers in your life that should overlap the same way.
