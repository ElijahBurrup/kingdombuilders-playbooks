A Playbook On The Long Game

In 1911, two teams raced across the bottom of the world to reach the South Pole. Same ice. Same cold. Same goal. One marched a steady set distance every single day, in good weather and bad. The other sprinted when it was clear and hid when it was not.

One team came home. The other froze on the way back, eleven miles from the food that would have saved them.

This is not really a story about the South Pole. It is a story about how you are spending your life.

The arrow only carries what the draw put into it.

The Bow

All the power in an arrow is loaded by the slow, hard pull. Your steady, boring work is the draw. The win is just letting go.

Two Kinds of Things

Some things pay you back a little every day and stack up. Others ignore you for ages, then pay off all at once. Bring the wrong energy to the wrong one and you lose.

Three

Don't Get Knocked Out

Never make the mistake you do not come back from. Your steady work has one job above winning: keep you in the game long enough for your moment to arrive.

Four

The Trap in the Middle

The worst place to stand: working too hard to rest, not betting enough to win. It feels responsible. That is what makes it a trap. A short tool helps you find yours.

Five

How the same idea plays out in fitness, money, work, relationships, and health, so you can act on it today.

And when your clean shot comes, you will not have to hope you are ready. You will already be.
