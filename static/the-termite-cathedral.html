Chapter One

"The Mound's Mystery"

A 17-foot structure that regulates its own temperature to within one degree, built by blind insects the size of rice grains. No architect, no blueprint, no foreman. Why the most complex structures in the animal kingdom explain the most important shift in software history.

Chapter Two

"The Termite's Rule"

Five rules. Pick up soil. Follow the signal. Drop at the strongest point. Mark your drop. Repeat. That's the entire algorithm — for both termite mounds and Large Language Models. The structural parallel between stigmergy and token prediction that explains why AI "understands" code without understanding anything.

Chapter Three

"The Colony's Architecture"

Queen, soldiers, workers, farmers — four castes that never cross into each other's territory, yet produce a living city. How the termite colony maps directly onto modern AI agent systems: orchestrator, tester, coder, deployer. Why no single agent needs to understand the whole system.

Chapter Four

"The Cathedral Rises"

Humans build software top-down: plan, then code. AI agents build bottom-up: draft, test, fail, fix, repeat. It looks like brute force. It IS brute force. And it works — for the same reason a termite mound works. Because convergence beats elegance.

Chapter Five

"The Displacement Myth"

Weavers smashed the Jacquard loom — then quadrupled in number within a generation. ATMs were supposed to kill tellers — teller jobs doubled. Eight revolutions with real employment data that prove the same pattern: displacement creates more jobs than it destroys. Every single time.
