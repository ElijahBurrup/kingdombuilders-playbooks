Six Chapters That Rewire How You Eat

Chapter One

"The First Sin Was a Meal"

Genesis 3 and Matthew 4. The pattern of every temptation you face was written in the first three chapters of the Bible. Eve saw, desired, and ate. Jesus starved for forty days and still said no. The battleground has always been food.

Chapter Two

"The Three Tables"

There are only three tables in the universe. God's table, the table of indulgence, and the enemy's table. Every single bite seats you at one of them. The table is determined by the heart, not the menu.

Chapter Three

"The Birthright Bowl"

Esau was not starving. He was uncomfortable. And that discomfort cost him everything. Name what you are trading for a single bowl of stew. Then decide if the trade is worth it.

Chapter Four

"The Temple Inspection"

Jesus flipped tables and drove out merchants with a whip. What would He find if He inspected your temple's kitchen? Daniel was a teenager in captivity and refused the king's food. What is your reason for not refusing?

Chapter Five

"The Bread of Life"

You eat worst when you are emotionally hungry, not physically hungry. You are trying to feed a spiritual hunger with physical bread. Five loaves, two fish, five thousand people. Jesus looked up, blessed, broke, gave. Abundance overflowed.

Chapter Six

"The Daily Altar"

Three meals a day. Three acts of worship. The Last Supper was not a monthly ritual. It was a pattern for every meal. Seven principles that turn your plate into an altar and your kitchen into a sanctuary.

"Every meal is worship. Not metaphorically. Literally. You are either worshiping God, worshiping your appetite, or worshiping the enemy's counterfeit comfort. There is no neutral eating."
