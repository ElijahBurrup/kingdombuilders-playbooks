Before We Begin

"The Green Lens"

Envy doesn't announce itself. It disguises itself as admiration, motivation, even holy discontent. But underneath, it's a constant measurement — their highlight reel against your behind-the-scenes. This playbook makes the lens visible.

Chapters 1–2

"The Scroll" & "The Comparison Fast"

Every scroll is a courtroom. You didn't open your phone to browse — you opened it to measure. This pair identifies the altar of comparison scrolling and gives you a 7-day digital protocol to break the reflex of measuring your life against a curated feed.

Chapters 3–4

"The Applause That Burns" & "The Generosity Drill"

You said "congratulations" but something inside contracted. This pair exposes the inability to genuinely celebrate others and gives you the Response Audit, the 48-Hour Generosity Window, and the Amplification Practice to retrain the reflex.

Chapter 5

"The Scarcity Lie"

Envy depends on one belief: that God's provision is limited. That their blessing costs you yours. This bridge chapter dismantles the zero-sum theology that makes every win around you feel like your loss — and installs the Abundance Reframe.

Chapters 6–7

"The Stolen Assignment" & "The Assignment Protocol"

You're not jealous of their house. You're jealous of their calling. This pair names the deepest altar — coveting someone else's assignment from God — and gives you the Lane Clarity Exercise, the Assignment Audit, and the Stewardship Shift to reclaim your own.

Chapter 8

"The Abundant Table"

"You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies." The table is not small. There is room. This closing chapter installs a Daily Abundance practice — naming three things that are yours and no one else's — for staying in your lane without resentment.

"Envy is the only sin that gives you absolutely nothing in return. Lust gives pleasure. Greed gives accumulation. Wrath gives adrenaline. Envy just takes your peace and hands you someone else's scoreboard."

The Core Revelation

Envy is not about wanting what they have. It's about believing God forgot about you. Every comparison is a theology statement: "Your provision is not enough." This playbook doesn't teach you to stop wanting — it teaches you to stop measuring. Because the table God set for you has no competitors.
