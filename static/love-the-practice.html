Six Reps For Working With AI Like A Master

The Magic Trick

Stephen Curry walks onto the court, picks up a ball at half court, takes one dribble, releases. The ball travels forty-seven feet. Before it lands, Curry has already turned away. The shot goes in. The audience sees magic. Curry sees rep fifty thousand.

A new arena is rising in front of you. It is called working with AI. Inside it, a generation is quietly accumulating three million reps. Five years from now, those people will look like wizards to everyone who waited to feel inspired.

This playbook is the rep sheet. Six fundamentals. One master per fundamental, drawn from people who built superpowers by drilling boring things for a long time. One specific daily drill for AI work per fundamental. Each drill takes ten to fifteen minutes. The point is not day one. The point is day three hundred and sixty five.

The Six Reps Inside

01

Taste · Stephen Curry

Daily drill:Generate the same output three times. Rank them. Name why the best one beat the others. The vocabulary of why is the rep that builds the eye that fires before the result lands.

02

Naming · Jiro Ono

Daily drill:Take one fuzzy thing you want. Compress to 25 words. Then 15. Then 5. The compression IS the rep. The model only knows what you say.

03

Decomposition · Roger Bannister

Daily drill:Find the smallest atomic step the AI can fully complete in one turn. Chain five of them. Bannister did not run a mile. He ran four perfect quarters.

04

Iteration · Pixar

Daily drill:Take a result you got in one turn yesterday. Refine it through ten turns today. The work is at turn ten, not turn one. Pixar shipped on draft twenty-seven.

05

Synthesis · Lin-Manuel Miranda

Daily drill:Generate the same thing three ways. Highlight the single strongest element in each. Combine. Hamilton was three known things synthesized. The combination is the originality.

06

Verification · Hubble Telescope Team

Daily drill:Three anchors per output. One factual claim. One number. One logical leap. Verify each. Fluent is not the same as true.

What You Will Have

By day 30, a vocabulary of imperfection (the taste lexicon Jiro spent ten years on rice to build). By day 90, the naming reflex that turns generic prompts into surgical ones. By day 180, the iteration discipline that takes you past turn ten where the magic lives. By day 365, the synthesis instinct that produces work nobody else could have made because nobody else ran your specific combination.
