What's Inside

The Chapters

From the first saved acorn to the crash that changes everything -- the full economic cycle, told through fur and bark.

Chapter 1
"The Saver and the Builder"

Hazel has 200 acorns she doesn't need right now. Chester has a plan for a winter-proof storehouse but only 50 acorns to his name. When their paths cross, something remarkable happens: Hazel's patience becomes Chester's opportunity.

This is how credit is born. Not from banks. Not from governments. From one squirrel trusting another.

The Oak Vault
Interest Emerges

Penny opens the forest's first bank. She pays Hazel for depositing acorns. She charges Chester for borrowing them. The difference is her margin. Interest is the price of time -- the reward for patience and the cost of impatience.

The chapter that makes "interest rates" feel obvious instead of abstract.

Chapter 2
"The Credit Boom"

Elder Oaksworth, the forest council's wisest member, lowers the Oak Vault's rates to encourage building after a harsh winter. Borrowing surges. New storehouses go up. Acorn prices rise. Everyone feels wealthy.

Low interest rates feel like prosperity. But cheap credit has a shadow.

The Bubble
The Bushy Tail Brothers Arrive

They don't build storehouses. They buy prime tree plots, hold them for two months, and sell for double. Land flipping becomes normal. New borrowers use borrowed acorns to buy land at prices that only make sense if prices keep rising forever.

When borrowing is easy, everyone feels rich -- until they're not.

The Crash
When the Music Stops

One cold morning, no one shows up to buy the Bushy Tail Brothers' latest plot. The price drops. Then drops again. Borrowers can't repay. The Oak Vault's shelves are bare. Hazel's savings are at risk.

Recovery
Rebuilding from the Ashes

The forest doesn't end. The economy doesn't die. But it changes. New rules. New caution. And the quiet return of squirrels like Hazel -- savers who never stopped collecting, even when no one was watching.

Core Concept

The Ecosystem of Money

Savers, Builders, Bankers, Speculators

Every economy needs all four. Savers provide the fuel. Builders create the value. Bankers connect them. Speculators test the limits.

The playbook shows what happens when the balance tips -- when cheap credit turns builders into speculators, when bankers stop measuring risk, when savers get punished for patience.

Once you see the pattern in acorns, you will never unsee it in the real world.
