Chapter One

"The Weaver's Ceiling"

For 4,000 years, the fastest way to weave a complex pattern was a human with a perfect memory. The ceiling never moved. Why the weaver was always the bottleneck — and why programmers in 2019 were hitting the exact same wall.

Joseph Marie Jacquard, 1804. Punch cards that separated the pattern from the practitioner for the first time in human history. Why this moment — not the steam engine, not the printing press — is the true origin of the software revolution.

Charles Babbage saw a loom and imagined a computer. The category leap from specific patterns to general-purpose computation — and the identical leap that happened when LLMs went from autocomplete to reasoning.

"The Agent's Loom"

The loom is weaving its own patterns now. How AI agents plan, write, test, debug, and deploy entire applications — and why this is not replacing developers but removing the ceiling, just like Jacquard did for weavers.
