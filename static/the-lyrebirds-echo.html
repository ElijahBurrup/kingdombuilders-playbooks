You type a sentence. Thirty seconds later, a full song plays back. Vocals, drums, melody, mix. But no musician touched an instrument. No producer opened a session. How does sound come from text?

A lyrebird learns every sound in the forest. Chainsaws, camera shutters, other birds, even human speech. She copies none of them. She reassembles them into something new. That is exactly how AI music generation works.

Four Chapters

What You Will Learn

Chapter 01 — The Forest
How AI Learns Music
Training on millions of songs. Breaking sound into tokens, learning patterns of melody, rhythm, and genre. The lyrebird listens to the entire forest before she sings a single note.
Chapter 02 — The Stage
How Sound Becomes a Picture
Spectrograms: turning waveforms into visual maps of frequency and time. The bat reads sound the way you read a page. This is the secret bridge between audio and image AI.
Chapter 03 — The Ping
How Noise Becomes Music
Diffusion: starting with static and subtracting noise, one careful step at a time, until a clean spectrogram emerges. The model does not compose. It reveals.
Chapter 04 — The Audience
How Text Steers Sound
Prompts, latent space, text conditioning, and the vocoder. How a sentence like "upbeat jazz with female vocals" becomes a finished song in thirty seconds.
