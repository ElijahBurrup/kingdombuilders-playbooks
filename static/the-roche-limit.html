What's Inside

Five Chapters Across Three Scientific Domains

Chapter One

"The Shattering"

In 1848, Edouard Roche proved that every moon has a minimum safe distance from its planet. Get closer and the moon does not merge. It disintegrates into a ring of fragments orbiting the planet forever. Saturn's rings are the debris of moons that crossed the line. Your fragments are orbiting someone else's center right now.

Chapter Two

"The Hijack"

The barnacle Sacculina injects itself into a crab's bloodstream, grows roots through its entire nervous system, and reprograms the crab to nurture the parasite's eggs as its own. The crab does not know. It feels like purpose. This chapter reveals whose goals you have actually been serving, and why it felt like love.

Chapter Three

"The Immune Response"

In autoimmune disease, the body attacks its own healthy tissue because the self/non-self recognition system has failed. This is why guilt floods you the moment you prioritize yourself. Your psychological immune system tags your own needs as threats. The guilt is real. It is aimed at the wrong target.

Chapter Four

"The Oxygen"

With oxygen, one glucose molecule produces 36 ATP. Without it, the same glucose produces 2 ATP plus toxic byproducts. The ratio is 18 to 1. Boundaries are not walls. They are oxygen. The resentment, exhaustion, and passive aggression in your relationship are not character flaws. They are lactic acid. The chemistry of love without air.

Chapter Five

"The Density"

A neutron star can orbit a black hole at a distance that would annihilate any ordinary star. Not because it is bigger. Because it is denser. The Roche limit depends on your density, not your distance. This chapter gives you five practices to increase your self-cohesion until you can survive the closeness you actually want.

"The goal is not distance. The goal is density. Two dense bodies can orbit millimeters apart without breaking. Two diffuse ones shatter across the room."
